4Peaks Edit Menu

Copying of Selections

The default shortcut keys for copying, ⌘+C, work in 4Peaks, but by default it will copy your DNA sequence. It will also copy your sequence when the 'Sequence' tab is selected in the drawer. When the 'Translation' tab is selected, 4Peaks will copy the translation. Copying of sequences and translations is also possible via the shortcut keys ⌘+shift+C and ⌘+shift+T respectively.


Editing Bases

Sequence files might not be perfect because of a lot of possibilities, but some imperfections, like a T peak, will not distort your complete sequence and you will be able to call the bases yourself. In 4Peaks you can edit, insert, and delete bases. You are also able to crop your sequence to the region you are interested in, or to the region which is still readable.

Because sequence reactions can done with sense and anti-sense primers, the results will also be generated in a sense and a reverse complement.

Remark: Sequence files opened in ABI, ALF, CTF and BIO format cannot be saved as such files again after editing.


Reverse complement

For sequences obtained with reverse primers, the reverse option can be used to get a reverse complement sequence. The bases and the peaks are all transformed to the complementary code and color.


BLAST

To BLAST your obtained sequence against the NCBI database, choose the BLAST option in the edit menu. Your sequence will either be copied to the clipboard (Blast 2 sequences), or pasted into the sequence field of the BLAST against a selected genome (e.g. MegaBLAST).


Remark: Most of the editing actions described here have shortcut keys as indicated below and can also be found under the Action button of the main window (Figure 1).
